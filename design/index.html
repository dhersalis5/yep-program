The journey · 20 sessions over one semester

First you learn.
Then you build.

Be clear on what this is: the first half is real learning — workshops, fundamentals and guest founders. You don't launch a startup on day one. You earn the tools first, then put them to work.

First half · ~2.5 months

Foundations & theory

Weekly classes on the fundamentals, taught by rotating parent-entrepreneurs, plus an outside guest sharing their story every few weeks. This is where you build the muscle.

Second half · ~2.5 months

Build your startup

Now you apply it. Intense work in and out of class — problem, idea and resources — with two mentors guiding your team all the way to the pitch.

No false expectations: several weeks are dedicated to learning before any building begins. The payoff is that when you do build, you build something real.
